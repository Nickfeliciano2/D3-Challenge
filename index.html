Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks vs Age/Income/and Poverty

Looking at the data, it appears Obesity has a negative sloped correlation as the states with a higher percentile of obesity are also the lowest household incomes. Obesity hits mostly around the late 30’s, and the more impoverished states have the higher obese percent’s as well.

Smoking seems to have the same trends as obesity, poorer household Income states seem to have more smokers. Obesity is a higher percentile though, with smoking ranging in the 10-30% range while obesity is at the 22-40% range in all states.

It seems that Lacking healthcare is even across the ranges, as the % varies and does not appear to have a correlation. It does seem to trend that the poorer you are the more likely you are to not have healthcare, but it does not appear that age affects this very much.
